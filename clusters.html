✨ Star Clusters

Star clusters are groups of stars that were formed together from the same giant cloud of gas and dust and held together by gravity. Since they were born at the same time, they are usually of similar age and composition. This makes them very useful for scientists who want to study how stars develop.

There are two main kinds of clusters. Some are small and loosely spread out, while others are large and tightly packed with thousands or even millions of stars. These clusters can be found in different parts of a galaxy.

In a star cluster, the stars are held together by gravity. Over time, some stars may slowly drift away, but many remain together for billions of years. When seen from Earth, clusters can appear as a bright patch or a group of closely placed stars.

By studying star clusters, astronomers learn how stars are born, how they age, and how they eventually die.

Types:

  • Open Clusters- Open clusters are small, loosely packed groups of young stars formed from the same cloud of gas and dust. They are usually found in the spiral arms of galaxies and may slowly drift apart over time.
  • Globular Clusters- Globular clusters are large, tightly packed groups of very old stars, often containing thousands to millions of stars. They are found in the outer regions of galaxies and have a spherical shape due to strong gravity.

Example: The Pleiades

Fun Fact:

  • The Pleiades cluster is also called the “Seven Sisters”.
  • Some globular clusters contain more than a million stars!
  • Star clusters help scientists understand how old our galaxy is.
  • The closest star cluster to Earth is the Hyades cluster.
  • Many clusters can be seen with binoculars.
