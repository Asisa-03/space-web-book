🌍 Galaxies

A galaxy is a massive system of stars, planets, gas, and dust. Galaxies are the largest structures in the universe. Each galaxy is a huge system filled with stars, planets, gas, dust, and invisible matter, all held together by gravity. Some galaxies are small with a few million stars, while others are massive and contain trillions.

Galaxies come in many shapes. Some have long spiral arms filled with stars and dust. Others are round or oval, and some have no clear shape at all. Inside each galaxy, stars are born, live their lives, and eventually die, helping to create new stars again.

Our Solar System is part of a galaxy that contains billions of stars. The Sun is just one among them. The distance between galaxies is extremely large, so they appear as tiny faint spots in the night sky.

Scientists study galaxies to understand how the universe began and how it continues to grow. When we observe distant galaxies, we are actually looking back in time because their light takes millions of years to reach us.

Our home galaxy is the Milky Way.

Types:

  • Spiral galaxies- Spiral galaxies have a flat disk with curved arms spreading out from a bright center. These arms contain gas, dust, and young stars where new stars are continuously formed. Our galaxy, the Milky Way, is a spiral galaxy.
  • Elliptical galaxies- Elliptical galaxies are round or oval in shape and do not have spiral arms. They mostly contain older stars and very little gas or dust, so new star formation happens less often.
  • Irregular galaxies- Irregular galaxies have no definite shape and look uneven or scattered. They often form this way due to collisions or strong gravitational pulls from nearby galaxies and usually have active star formation.

    • Fun Fact:

    • There are billions of galaxies in the universe.
    • The nearest large galaxy to us is the Andromeda Galaxy.
    • The Milky Way and Andromeda will collide in about 4 billion years!
    • Some galaxies are much bigger than ours and contain trillions of stars.
    • Galaxies can spin, and it takes our galaxy about 225 million years to complete one rotation.
