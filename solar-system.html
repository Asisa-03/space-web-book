☀️ Solar System

The Solar System is a vast family of objects that are held together by the gravity of the Sun. The Sun is the center of the Solar System and provides heat and light. Everything in it — planets, moons, asteroids, and comets — moves around the Sun in a path called an orbit. The closer a planet is to the Sun, the faster it travels.

The planets in the inner part of the Solar System are rocky and smaller. These include Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. The outer planets are much larger and mostly made of gas and ice. These include Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Each planet has its own temperature, atmosphere, and number of moons.

Apart from planets, there are many smaller bodies. Asteroids are rocky objects that mainly exist in a belt between Mars and Jupiter. Comets are made of ice and dust and develop bright tails when they come close to the Sun. Meteors are small pieces of rock that burn up when they enter Earth’s atmosphere.

Scientists study the Solar System to understand how planets form and how life began on Earth. Space missions and satellites continue to explore different planets and send back valuable information.

Planets:

Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune

Fun Fact:

  • A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus!
  • Mercury has no atmosphere, so temperatures change very quickly.
  • Jupiter has a giant storm called the Great Red Spot that has been raging for over 300 years.
  • Saturn’s rings are made mostly of ice and rock.
  • The Solar System formed about 4.6 billion years ago.
  • The asteroid belt lies between Mars and Jupiter.
  • Neptune has the strongest winds in the Solar System.
