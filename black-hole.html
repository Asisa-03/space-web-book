🕳️ Black Holes

A black hole is a region in space where gravity is so strong that nothing can escape from it, not even light. Black holes are formed when extremely large stars reach the end of their life and collapse under their own gravity. When this happens, all their mass gets squeezed into a tiny space, creating a region where gravity becomes incredibly powerful.

The boundary around a black hole is called the event horizon. Once anything crosses this boundary, it cannot escape. Even light cannot come out, which is why black holes appear completely dark.

Although black holes cannot be seen directly, scientists can detect them by studying their effects on nearby objects. When gas, dust, or stars get pulled toward a black hole, they heat up and emit strong energy before disappearing. This helps scientists know that a black hole is present.

Some black holes are small, while others are extremely large and lie at the centers of galaxies. These giant black holes may play an important role in how galaxies grow and change over time.

Fun Fact:

  • Black holes cannot be seen directly.
  • At the center of our galaxy, the Sagittarius A* is a supermassive black hole.
  • Black holes can “eat” stars that come too close.
  • When matter falls into a black hole, it heats up and glows brightly before disappearing.
  • Scientists captured the first real image of a black hole in 2019.
  • Black holes can bend space and time around them!
